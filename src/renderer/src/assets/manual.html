What AIHub Browser is

AIHub Browser is a Chromium-based desktop browser with an AI agent wired into its core. It browses the web like Chrome, organizes knowledge like Obsidian, and acts on your behalf like an assistant that can actually click things.

It runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux, built on Electron and Chromium — so every site that works in Chrome works here, including Google sign-in.

Agentic

The AI can open tabs, read pages, fill forms, search the live web, write files, and build whole projects — with your approval at every risky step.

Visual

Bookmarks live in a force-directed constellation you can fly through, search, and replay as a timelapse.

Private

Local AI via Ollama, a free built-in VPN, encrypted tokens, and keys that never leave your machine.

The interface, labeled

Four zones. Learn these and everything else is discoverable.

1 · Tab strip

Drag to reorder, drag out to detach into its own window, right-click for the full tab menu. Scrolls horizontally when full; the active tab always stays visible.

2 · Navigation + address bar

Back, forward, reload, home, then the omnibox. On the right: bookmark, annotate, screenshot, record, and the AI button.

3 · Sidebar

Toggle with the panel icon. Home, Research, Agent Mode, Sticky Notes, History, Downloads, Extensions, Wi-Fi, VPN, Mail, Settings.

4 · AI panel

Docked on the right, resizes the page rather than covering it. Chat, quick actions, and live agent step tracking.

Your first five minutes

This really is a sequence — do it in order and the rest of the app makes sense.

  1. Connect an AI brain. Sidebar → Settings → AI. Paste an OpenRouter key for cloud models (Claude, GPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, Grok, Mistral), or install Ollama and pick a local model for fully private AI. The header of the AI panel tells you which one is live.
  2. Open the assistant. Press Ctrl+Shift+A and ask it something real: research the best budget laptops and give me a table.
  3. Bookmark a few pages. Press Ctrl+D on any site. The AI auto-categorizes and colors each one.
  4. Open the Sphere. Sidebar → Home → sphere view. Hit Timelapse and watch your graph build itself.
  5. Make it yours. Settings → Appearance for 25 themes, transparency, and window opacity.

The AI assistant

This is the part that makes AIHub different from every other browser. It is not a chatbot bolted onto a sidebar — it can act.

Open it with Ctrl+Shift+A or the violet AI button. The header shows which brain is answering: ● Local · Ollama or ● Cloud · OpenRouter.

What it can do

Research the live web

Searches the real internet, opens the best sources, reads them, and synthesizes an answer with citations. It never has to say "I can't browse."

Drive the browser

Opens, closes, and navigates tabs. Reads any page's text. Scans forms, fills fields, clicks buttons.

Write & build

Generates code, documents, and whole working projects into a folder you choose — then installs, runs, and fixes them until they pass.

Handle your files

Reads local documents (including .docx), improves them, and delivers results via a save dialog.

Summarize & explain

One-click page summaries you can export as Markdown or file into your Articles bookmarks.

Know your context

It sees your bookmarks, recent history, and current page, so "open my trading dashboard" just works.

Beautifully formatted answers new

Ask for a comparison and you get a real table — not a wall of bullets. Ask for code and you get a syntax block with a copy button. Links arrive titled and clickable, opening in a new tab.

Three ways to give it context

  • Select text, then right-click → Ask AI about "…" — pre-fills the question with your selection.
  • Attach Page — the quick-action button loads the whole page into the conversation so you can interrogate it.
  • Summarize — one click; afterwards you can export the summary as .md or save it to Articles.
Safety rails you should know about. The agent asks permission before running any shell command, and it will never click a final Submit / Apply / Send button on its own — it stops, shows you exactly what's about to be sent, and waits for your confirmation. If a site needs a login, a CAPTCHA, or a file upload, it hands control back to you. Press the ■ stop button any time to halt a running task.
Getting better answers: say the output shape you want ("as a table", "5 bullets", "a bash script"), name the constraint ("under $500", "published this year"), and tell it when to use real sources ("with citations"). It will use live web search whenever the question needs current facts.

The command palette

Press Ctrl+K anywhere. Start typing and results filter live across five groups:

  • Actions — new tab, reopen closed tab, toggle the AI, annotate, read aloud, find in page, add to Sphere, compare two pages.
  • Go to — every built-in page: Research, Agent, Notes, Rewind, Watch & Ping, History, Downloads, Extensions, Wi-Fi, VPN, Mail, Manual, Settings.
  • Tabs — jump straight to any open tab by title.
  • Bookmarks — open anything in your Sphere.
  • Search / open — if nothing matches, Enter treats what you typed as a URL or a web search.
Everything is keyboard-first: ↑/↓ to move, Enter to run, Esc to close.

Rewind — search what you’ve read

Open Rewind from the sidebar (or the command palette). It keeps a private, on-device index of the pages you genuinely spent time reading — after a few seconds of dwell, the page’s text is captured. Nothing leaves your machine.

Type a phrase you half-remember and Rewind ranks matches by how well the words fit, weighting titles and recent visits, and shows a snippet with your terms highlighted. Click a result to reopen the page. Remove single entries or clear the whole index from the same screen.

Rewind only captures real reading — quick redirects and pages under a sentence or two are skipped, so the index stays useful.

Watch & Ping

Let a page tell you when it changes, instead of you checking it fifty times.

Open Watch & Ping from the sidebar, hit New watch, and paste the page URL. Choose what counts as news:

  • When it changes at all — AIHub fingerprints the page’s text and pings you if anything meaningful shifts.
  • When a word appears — give it a phrase like In stock, Sold, or Closed, and it only pings when that phrase shows up.

Pick how often to re-check — every 15 minutes up to once a day — and AIHub keeps checking quietly in the background, even while you browse elsewhere. When it fires, you get a real desktop notification; click it to jump straight to the page.

Bible

The whole Bible, bundled into the app and rendered as a real book you turn with your hand — no internet connection required.

Open Bible from the sidebar or the command palette (Ctrl+K). All 66 books are built into AIHub itself, so the reader opens and reads even with no network connection at all.

The text is the World English Bible (WEB), a modern-English translation that is fully public domain — free of the licensing that covers translations like the NIV, NKJV, or ESV.

The Bible opens as a two-page spread, like a real book. Pick any book from the dropdown, step through it one spread at a time with the Prev / Next buttons, or turn a page by hand:

Selecting and marking a verse

Click any verse to select it. A small action bar appears at the bottom of the screen:

Highlight

Five colors — yellow, green, blue, pink, purple. Click a color to paint the verse, click the same color again to remove it.

Save

Bookmarks the verse; the icon fills in once it's saved. Everything you save is listed under Saved at the top right of the reader.

Note

Opens a note editor, pre-filled with any note you already left on that verse. Save keeps it, Clear note deletes it, and Esc or a click outside closes it without changing anything.

Share

Opens the share sheet, covered below.

A verse carrying a note shows a small dot next to its verse number, so you can spot your notes as you read.

The Saved button in the reader's header opens your bookmarked verses. Click one to jump straight to it — the reader opens that book and spread and selects the verse — or remove it from the list with the bin icon.

Highlights, saved verses, notes, and the book and chapter you were last reading are all saved to your machine and are still there the next time you open the app.

Sharing a verse

The share sheet offers Facebook, X, WhatsApp, LinkedIn, Telegram, Reddit, and Email — each opens the platform in your system browser with the verse and its reference pre-filled. Copy puts the quote and reference on your clipboard.

TikTok and Instagram don't accept a shared link at all, so they're not in that row. Use Save image instead — it renders the verse onto a shareable image and downloads it as a PNG, ready for you to post yourself.

A handful of verses look empty

Luke 17:36, Acts 8:37, Acts 15:34, Acts 24:7, and Romans 16:25 show a small italic note instead of verse text. The WEB keeps these traditional verse numbers even though the passages themselves are absent from the oldest manuscripts — most modern translations quietly renumber around them. It's expected, not a bug.

Focus sessions & tab sleeping

Focus sessions

On the homepage, start a focus sprint and list the sites that derail you. For the length of the timer AIHub actually blocks them — try to open one and you land on a calm “come back later” page instead. When the timer ends, the block lifts on its own.

Tab sleeping

Tabs you haven’t touched in a while go to sleep and hand their memory back to the system, so a wall of open tabs stops slowing you down. A sleeping tab wakes the instant you click it. Want it now? Right-click any tab → Sleep Tab (free memory).

Per-site AI memory pairs well here: open the assistant on a site, hit the green Memory button, and jot standing instructions the AI should always apply on that site.

Cross-device handoff

Move your whole browsing session to your other computer in two clicks — through your own Google account, with nobody in the middle.

On the machine you’re leaving, open the command palette (Ctrl+K) and run Send my tabs to another device. Every open web page is bundled up and stored in a private, hidden folder inside your Google Drive.

On the other machine — signed into the same Google account and running AIHub — open the palette and run Open tabs from another device. Your tabs reopen exactly as they were, and the handed-off copy is cleared automatically.

StepWhere
Send my tabs to another deviceCommand palette (Ctrl+K), on the machine you’re leaving
Open tabs from another deviceCommand palette, on the machine you’re moving to
Handoff uses Google Drive’s app-data space — a hidden per-app folder that never shows up in your Drive and that no other app can read. Connect Google in Settings (with Drive enabled) on each device once, and it just works. Nothing passes through any server we run.

The Bookmark Sphere

About the animation

When the sphere opens, nodes don't just appear. The most-connected hubs pop in first on a spring that slightly overshoots and settles, mid-tier nodes follow, then the leaves — while edges draw themselves outward from source to target with a bright travelling tip. The camera dollies from pulled-back to fitted. Once settled, every node breathes gently, hubs a little more than leaves.

Timelapse takes that idea further: it empties the canvas and rebuilds your graph one node at a time in order of importance, roughly eight nodes a second, with a live progress bar. An edge only appears once both of its endpoints exist, and the physics keeps reflowing as the graph grows — so you watch your knowledge base assemble itself. Press Stop to bring everything back instantly.

Sticky notes that actually come back new

Click New Note in the annotation toolbar to pin a note to the page. Give it a title, type freely, pick from six pastel colors, drag it anywhere, minimize it.

Free VPN

No account, no subscription, no credentials. Sidebar → VPN, click a country, done.

Available locations: France, Belgium, Canada, United States, United Kingdom, Germany, Netherlands, Japan — plus Direct to turn it off.

How it works

  1. You click a country.
  2. The browser pulls a fresh list of free community servers for that country.
  3. It tests them in batches, and only accepts one that both responds and genuinely changes your public IP.
  4. The card shows live progress ("Testing 7/40"). Tap again to cancel.
Be realistic about free servers. They're community-run: speeds vary, servers come and go, and the first connection to a country can take up to half a minute while the browser hunts for a working one. Don't do banking through one. If a server drops, just reconnect and it finds another.

Have your own paid proxy? Advanced → Add your own proxy server supports SOCKS5, HTTP, and HTTPS with optional credentials.

Wi-Fi

Sidebar → Wi-Fi scans every network around you and lets you join any of them — open or secured.

  • Open networks connect with one click, no password.
  • Secured networks prompt for the password once, then Windows remembers it.
  • Saved networks show a Saved badge and reconnect in one click.
  • Real verification — the browser waits and confirms you actually associated, rather than claiming success immediately. A wrong password is reported honestly and the bad profile is cleaned up.
Wi-Fi management is Windows-only for now. On any open network, turn the VPN on before doing anything sensitive.

Mail, Drive & Calendar

Sidebar → Mail gives you a native Gmail client. Connect through Settings → Google, which uses OAuth2 with PKCE and opens consent in your system browser — AIHub never sees your Google password.

  • Read threads, search, and manage labels.
  • Drive and Calendar use the same connection.
  • Tokens are encrypted at rest on your machine.
First-time setup needs a Google Desktop OAuth client ID pasted into Settings. Until that's filled in, the Mail page will tell you it can't connect.

Privacy & security

  • Local AI option — with Ollama, nothing you type leaves your machine.
  • Keys stay local — API keys are stored on your device, never transmitted anywhere but the provider you chose.
  • OAuth done properly — Google sign-in uses PKCE in your system browser; tokens are encrypted at rest.
  • Web security stays on — pages run with full Chromium sandboxing.
  • Prompt-injection defense — text from pages, files, and command output is fed to the AI as data, and can't issue it commands.
  • Command approval — nothing executes on your system without an explicit click.

AIHub checks GitHub Releases for updates on startup and periodically, and tells you in-app when a new version ships.
